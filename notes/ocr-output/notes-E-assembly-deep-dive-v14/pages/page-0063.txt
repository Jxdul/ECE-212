E.5.5 The conditional IT (if-then) block
IT{x{y{z}}}
CC
Makes the (1 to 4) following instructions conditional, based on the specified cc. The
conditions may all be the same, or some may be the logical inverse of cc.
• X, y, z specify conditions for the 2nd /3rd / 4th instructions in the IT block; each
is:
• T (then) = the base condition is applied
• E (else) = the inverse of the base condition is applied
e.g., ITTE GT
• The IT block gives us a way to implement simple IF-THEN-ELSE structures
without requiring as many conditional and unconditional branching instruc-
tions.
• A BKPT (breakpoint) in an IT block always executes regardless of its condition.
• Exceptions can occur within the IT block.
• Can use AL (always) as base condition, but then may not use any E (else) con-
ditions.
