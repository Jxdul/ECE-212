G.2 Learning objectives
By the end of this section of the course, you should be able to:
• Define, in your own words, the terms active-high, active-low, asserted, negated,
tri-state.
• Explain what it means for devices to be memory-mapped.
• Describe the relationship between memory port size, data bus size, and explain
how any particular byte, halfword, or word is accessed.
• Read and interpret timing diagrams for various hardware peripherals.
• Define direct memory access; explain its advantages and describe its basic oper-
ation.
• Explain, in your own words, what full and partial address decoding are.
• Explain how partial decoding leads to multiple copies being mapped into mem-
ory space; discuss advantages and disadvantages.
• Explain the relationship between chip select signals and address decoding; de-
scribe the general goals for the design of the address decoder.
