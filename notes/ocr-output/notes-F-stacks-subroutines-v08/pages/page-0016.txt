F.4.5 Primary uses of the stack
• (in many CPUs, but not always for ARM • next page) saving PC when calling
a subroutine
• PC points to next instruction to be fetched and executed
• To call a subroutine, we must branch to the subroutine code, then come
back and continue with the next instruction.
• Thus, we need to save PC before calling the subroutine and restore PC after
the subroutine is done. One way is to save PC on the stack.
• branch to subroutine instruction
pushes PC onto stack,
then sets PC to address of the subroutine being called; e.g.,
PC < PC + displacement
• return from subroutine instruction
pops PC from the stack
2 saving and restoring values of registers, while doing calculations but wanting
not to change those registers' values.)
3 passing input and output parameters to / from subroutines
