F.6.5 Recoverability
Recoverability, also known as exception handling, is the ability of a program/-
module to:
• cope with erroneous data
• recover from certain classes of error
F.6.6 Putting it all together
Combining the components above,
• allows the avoidance of GOTO statements (generally a last resort in high-level
programming languages)
• resulting programs are:
• easier to understand
• easier to debug
• more elegant
