E.1 What's this all about?
• In this section, we embark on a tour of the ARMv7-M assembly language and
get into the nitty-gritty details.
• Our tour is organised by instruction category (data movement, arithmetic, ad-
dress handling, etc.) and presents commonly used operations to allow us to
implement assembly language programs.
• The big-picture objective is for you to obtain enough working knowledge that
will enable you to look up any instruction from suitable reference materials and
ain an understanding of what the instruction does and how it is encoded.
• Along the way, we will introduce new concepts that relate to programming
and some programming examples.

E.3.4 MOV (shifted register)
ARM's processor design includes a powerful barrel shifter that can perform a range
of useful shift/ rotate operations on the value read from the source operand.
• MOV {S}
Rd, Rm, < shift>
where shift is one of:
• LSL #k — logical shift left
• LSR #k — logical shift right
• ASR #K — arithmetic shift right
• ROR #k — rotate right
• RRX #k — rotate right with extend
Note: We'll use standalone instructions LSL, LSR, etc. instead of this MOV with
shifted register form.

LDR = load register (word)
Consider the LDR instruction. (PQR / Opcode reference / row 97)
There are three sub-categories:
• immediate — of the form LDR Rt, [Rn, #immed]
The immediate value refers to an optional fixed-value offset.
• literal — of the form LDR Rt, ‹label>
e.g., named variables defined with directives like DCB, DCD, etc.
• register — of the form LDR Rt, [Rn, Rm]
The offset is the value of a second register Rm.

E.5.5 The conditional IT (if-then) block
IT{x{y{z}}}
CC
Makes the (1 to 4) following instructions conditional, based on the specified cc. The
conditions may all be the same, or some may be the logical inverse of cc.
• X, y, z specify conditions for the 2nd /3rd / 4th instructions in the IT block; each
is:
• T (then) = the base condition is applied
• E (else) = the inverse of the base condition is applied
e.g., ITTE GT
• The IT block gives us a way to implement simple IF-THEN-ELSE structures
without requiring as many conditional and unconditional branching instruc-
tions.
• A BKPT (breakpoint) in an IT block always executes regardless of its condition.
• Exceptions can occur within the IT block.
• Can use AL (always) as base condition, but then may not use any E (else) con-
ditions.
